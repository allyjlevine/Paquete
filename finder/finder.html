Phone Applications

Imagine having to buy a wifi card, visit a wifi hotspot, and sit through slow internet speeds just to download Facebook or Uber. Cue el Paquete Semenal. Cubans can get new apps for there phone without going through the tiresome process necessary to gain access to the internet.

Music

Music is the most popular item in the packet. “Cubans love music," said Sergio, 31, a vendor of the packet. Many Cubans learn English through listening to songs from the United States and the UK. Businesses also use the packet. Walking in Havana, you can find many small cafes playing music videos on their television. Chances are you'll see a flashdrive plugged into the TV. If so, they got it from the packet.

Series

There aren’t many television shows produced in Cuba, so the packet provides a good option for Cubans interested in TV series. They can follow shows like Girls and Game of Thrones just like people elsewhere. Shows also stream on television but because television is run by the government, many scenes that are considered sexual or go against the beliefs of the government, like interactions between LGBTQ characters, are cut out.

Sports

A new packet is destributed to vendors each week with entirely different content from the week before. Cubans can stay up to date with sports by watching games right after they happen.

YouTube

Downloaded YouTube channels enable Cubans to stay in tune with a popular part of world culture: internet culture. When YouTube joined the packet, all of a sudden people were walking around Cuba wearing elaborate makeup that they learned from YouTube tutorials. The packet brings outside influences into the country.
